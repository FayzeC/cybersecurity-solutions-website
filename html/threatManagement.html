What is Threat Management?

Cyber threats are constantly changing and evolving, making them harder to detect. It can strike from anywhere - web sites, social media, email, servers and many more. Just installing a few security software or firewalls isn't going to protect your data from the hands of hackers. The key to having a successful security system is having a good threat management plan. Threat management allows us to identify threats earlier so that cybersecurity officers can make accurate decisions to respond to it as quickly as possible.

Threat Analysis

Threat analysis is a process of identifying and accessing the capabilities of your security systems against real-life cyber attacks. Here at CyberAlertz, our team utilizes threat intelligence to counter cyber attacks. Below are the three types of threat intelligence used in our services:

01    Strategic Threat Intelligence

This analysis is usually reserved for non-technical audiences to provide an overview of threat landscapes. Common sources used to gather information include policy documents, research reports and more. This helps clients better understand their defense systems and discuss with us what should be done.

02    Tactical Threat Intelligence

Tactical threat intelligence helps determine whether existing security programs will successfully mitigate risks. It allows our team to search and eliminate specific threats and is also used to speed up incident response.

03    Operational Threat Intelligence

In this analysis, our team uses knowledge about cyberattacks or events to give insights into the nature or timing of attacks. It uses details of past known cases as references to act more efficiently when issues arise.
