What is a Security Assessment?

Security assessments play a vital role in keeping your data safe as it proactively identifies vulnerabilities in your IT systems. It tests your organization's security preparedness and should be conducted at least once every two years. Carrying out various types of assessment helps you determine what to protect so you can develop the necessary IT security controls and strategies to mitigate risks.

Benefits

Easier Future Assessments

Having a good security assessment early on in the process makes future assessments much easier to update.

Reducing Long Term Cost

Identifying potential threats before it causes major damage can help your organization save money in the long run.

Identify Weakness

Helps you see which part of your security measures are relatively weak so you can formulate the most suitable strategy.

Network Penetration Testing

Network penetration testing is a simulated cyberattack against your network to uncover vulnerabilities and involves breaching of any number of systems internally or externally. Our penetration testing methodology includes attack simulations carried out by our network security professionals. The team will estimate the overall time according to your security needs as the overall time it takes to complete a network penetration test depends on the size and complexity of your network but with that said, most tests usually take one week to four weeks, start to finish.

  1. Planning and Information Gathering

    • Define the scope and goal of the test, including the testing methods to be used.
    • Gather information about in-scope targets to better understand its potential vulnerabilities. At this stage, penetration testers can identify additional information that may have been overlooked or not provided.

  2. Vulnerability Discovery

    • Attempt to gain access to data or resources through the target by using the latest hacking tools.

  3. Analysis and Exploitation

    • Analyze results to identify any sensitive data that was accessed, the duration a penetration tester was able to remain undetected in the system and other problems.
    • Formulate a plan to exploit these vulnerabilities in a safe way through manual techniques, cracking tools and much more.

  4. Final Review

    • Clients will be given a detailed report on where the testing was conducted, how vulnerabilities are found and exploited. It also includes the scope of the security testing, testing methodologies and findings.
    • Provide appropriate recommendations and security measures applicable to help clients fix these issues.

Web Application Penetration Testing

While network penetration tests focus more on the design, implementation, and maintenance of a network, web application penetration testing is aimed at apps and web servers. Even if you are up to date on software patches and security, cybercriminals are constantly evolving their methods.

The process of web application penetration tests is similar to network penetration tests where they go through the same 4 phases.

Let us introduce a few methods our team uses in this test:

  1. URL Manipulation

    • Attempt to access server file systems
    • Force a server to show unauthorized resources

  2. SQL Injection

    • Gain complete access to data in the server
    • Unauthorized viewing of personal information

  3. Denial-of-Service(DoS)

    • Overwhelm server to disrupt or paralyze services
    • Crash web servers

Risk Assessment

Risk assessment is the process of identifying risks and impacts and how to respond to them if it becomes a reality. It is an essential part of any business no matter the size and should be made a priority. Our team of experts at CyberAlertz helps you find these risks that you may not be aware of and deals with them.

The steps below outline how CyberAlertz conducts risk assessments.

01    Know Your System

Here is some information you should prepare before going on to the next step:

What kind of system do you use?

Who uses your system? List down every user if possible.

Where is the information stored?

How does information come and go?

02    Determine Risk and Impact

Our team consider risks such as unauthorized access and rate the impacts accordingly:

High - Major damage, unrecoverable files

Medium - Damaging but recoverable

Low - Minimal or non-existent impact

03    Calculate Likelihood

Take into account the type of vulnerability, the motivation of the hackers and the effectiveness of your security systems to determine the probability of an attack. Once all the possibilities are analyzed, it makes it easier to find which part of the system needs work.

04    Think of a Plan

Our team gathers all the information in a document and provides recommendations or services you should consider getting to strengthen your security system.
